Project:

Trying to shorten the process steps in the Background Information, the proposal is to:
  1. create a process to extract the array of Service Offerings and Service Requests from the TimebanksUSA Drupal database, format them into an appealing email, and communicate new Offers and Requests to members (like Groupon's daily emails) based on the member's expressed tolerance for email intrusions from the timebank;
  2. create a sub-process to schedule and execute task #1;
  3. create a sub-process to strip all personally identifying data from the extracted array of Service Offerings and Service Requests array, format the tailored result and post the results to the TOPS website as a marketing display of what members will find when they join the timebank; and
  4. create a flexible way for members to express their tolerance for email intrusions and modify that preference when desired. The proposed preference levels are, "Send me an email when:
    • when a new Offer or Request is saved in the database,
    • once per day when a new Offer or Request is saved in the database,
    • once per week when a new Offer or Request is saved in the database,
    • once per month when a new Offer or Request is saved in the database
    • never bother me with any email.

About:

Timebanks of Puget Sound (TOPS) helps strengthen community resilience through the exchange of skills and services among community members. Timebanks are sometimes referred to as alternative currency systems. TOPS currently has timebanks on the Eastside, Mercer Island, West Seattle, and soon Vashon Island.

TOPS web presence is a set of Wordpress websites (xxxx.tbanks.org) hosted by DreamHost that ultimately lead to the TimebanksUSA Drupal database. The TimebanksUSA database contains member data, Service Offerings and Service Requests, and accounting for each member's Time Credit balance.

One of the challenges with the current system is members must:
  1. remember the timebank,
  2. log in and then,
  3. search for Service Offerings or Service Requests to keep up with what is going on or what is new, then
  4. initiate a communication with the other member either offering or requesting a service that is of interest,
  5. negotiate parameters of the offer or request accomplishment,
  6. participate in the service exchange as the provider or recipient of services
  7. record the exchange in the recipient's account (minus # of hours service received) and the provider's account (plus # of hours service provided),
