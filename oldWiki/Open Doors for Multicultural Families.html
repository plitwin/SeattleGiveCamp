Background:
The mission of our organization, Open Doors for Multicultural Families, is to ensure that diverse families who have family members with developmental disabilities and special health care needs have equal access to culturally and linguistically appropriate information, resources, and services. We promote culturally competent services through community collaboration, training and partnerships. The organization was formed in June, 2009 by a diverse group of passionate parents of children with disabilities and professionals who care about people with disabilities and valued community volunteers after their collaborative work together for more than a decade.

Since then, we have grown from one full time volunteer position to one full time and seven part-time staff who are either bilingual or multilingual. Through 20+ languages spoken by our staff and volunteer board members, we were able to provide linguistically and culturally responsive services to more than 400 families of children with disabilities in 2012, 10 times increased in the number of family we serve since 2009. Majority of these immigrant and refugee families are low income, living in South Seattle and South King County where school districts have 100+ different languages spoken by their students’ families.

Over the past 4 years, Open Doors’ linguistically and culturally responsive services have been proven needed and valuable to our target population. Our quality services have resulted in many grant awards from recognizable funders, such as United Way of King County, Seattle Foundation, City of Seattle, Seattle People’s Fund, Office of Special Education Program of Federal Government and Washington State Attorney General’s Office.

As a new organization, growing rapidly in the past 4 years, from 1 staff in 2009 to 8 staff in 2013, we are in need of changing our current website to meet our growth needs. Currently, we conduct 15+ parent training workshops and family events in various languages annually. On average, each parent workshop we have 30-40 participants and more than 180 people attend each inclusive family event, including many valuable community volunteers. Handling registrations by papers have been time consuming for our staff. Also, we are planning to meet with more families’ training needs by posting training video clips on our website, or create webinar for families who aren’t able to attend face to face training. Because we are serving multicultural and multilingual families, we have plenty resources available in different languages. We would like to get them all organized in a way that is easy for navigation and topical search.


Changes made at Give Camp

  1. 1. Added live-translation features using Google API for any page. By adding the shorthand code [google-translator] to any post or page, a dropdown menu will be created which allows the user to select from a wide variety of languages to which the page could be translated. This even translates text in dropdown menus on the page. The translation seems to work pretty well in Hindi, Spanish, Japanese, and Chinese, but we have not yet done user-testing with speakers of other languages
  2. Added Gravity Forms to the website, which provides powerful tools for creating virtually any form imaginable. This also allows page admins to see a lost of all submissions to a form, and even download the data in a format readable by Microsoft Excel (.csv).
